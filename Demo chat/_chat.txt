[11/06/24, 16:54:07] Sitesh Goyal Civil IIT G: 2) A Modified Approach to Granulation of Data: Construction of Secondary Information Granules



Abstract

The conceptualization of Fuzzy clustering has augmented the field of Granular Computing  (GrC).  The construction of information granules, which are essentially the building blocks that integrate the whole concept, has been bettered as a result. These granules are further used for degranulation in order to obtain the granularized information.  This degranulation process might prove to be cumbersome in terms of the accuracy and the time taken. In this research, propose a unique approach to construct secondary granules in order to speed up the degranulation process while maintaining the accuracy of the same.  Define a family of ε-information granules (primary information granules) as a collection of hypercubes which represent the granulated information. These hypercubes are built around the cluster prototypes obtained from Fuzzy clustering. In addition, construct a family of secondary information granules concentric to these hypercubes. The correlation between the information and the secondary information granules is realized by the degree of similarity defined in this research. Furthermore, access the quality of these information granules by inspecting their coverage and specificity. The approach should pose certain real-world applications as well. The experimental results, carried out on certain datasets acquired from the UCI Machine Learning Repository, should be tabulated. Further insights regarding the construction of secondary information granules should be discussed.

Key Words: Granular Computing, Fuzzy clustering, Secondary Information Granules, Degree of similarity.

1. Project Purpose and Necessity

Query based searching for data can prove to be cumbersome in terms of time complexity when we are dealing with large datasets. Hence, a need arises to shorten this time-consuming process efficiently. Granular computing and other techniques can be utilized for this purpose, as stated in this report.

2. Proposed Solutions and Processes

1.     Cluster the data using Fuzzy C means clustering.

2.     Form information granules around the center prototypes.

3.     Use the proposed algorithm and construct secondary information granules by partitioning the primary granules.

4.     Construct the information granules and conceptualize the form that the information granule will take, wherein the computational power invested in degranulation and data retrieval is reduced by a significant amount.

 

3. Results

 The application of the proposed algorithm on 2-D synthetic data sets and data sets obtained from the Machine Learning Repository should be reported. Calculate the cluster centers using the FCM algorithm and proceed to form the information granules along with the partitions on the data set. Input in a query and the algorithm should return the closest approximation to the input query. Iterate over the values of ε to characterize the quality of coverage, specificity and Q and obtain the optimal value of ε. Set the value of fuzzifier m to 2.0 for both, FCM and the granulation process. Upon obtaining the optimal ε, plot the results of the granule formation over the set of data points. Also showcase the variation of Q and coverage over the number of clusters. Draw out the conclusions by varying the number of secondary information granules to be formed as well.
